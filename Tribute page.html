Mr. Nelson Mandela

The man who fought for equality of all mankind

Nelson Mandela(top row, second from left) on the steps of Wits University. (image:© Wits University Archives)

Here is a timeline of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Rolihlahla Mandela was born into the Madiba clan int the vilaage of Mvezo in the Eastern Cape, on 18 July, 1918.
  • 1930 - when he was 12 years old, 1930, his father died and the young Rolihlalhla became a ward of Jongintaba at the Great Place in Mqhekezweni.
  • 1941 - He was expelled from the University College of Fort Hare for participating in a student protest and a lot of other activities led to him running away to Johanesburb
  • 1943 - He completed his BA through the University of Southh Africa and went back to Fort Hare for his graduation in 1943
  • 1952 - He was chosen as the National Volunteer-in-Chief of the defiance Campaign with Maulvi Cachalia as his deputy
  • 1956 - Mandela was arrested in a countrywide police swoop on 5 December 1956, which led to the 1956 Treason Trial.
  • 1961 - He was asked to lead the armed struggle and helped to establish Umkhonto weSizwe.
  • 1989 - While in the last months of his imprisonment, he obtained an LLB through the University of South Africa. He graduated in absentia at a ceremony in Cape Town
  • 1994 - he was inaugurated as South Africa's first democratically elected president. in his 80th birthday.
  • 1998 - He married Graca Machel, his third wife.
  • 2013 - He died at his home in Johannesburg on 5th December, 2013
